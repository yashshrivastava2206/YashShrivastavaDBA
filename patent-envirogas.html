Abstract

ENVIROGAS is an innovative intelligent waste management system that integrates IoT technology with biogas extraction capabilities to address the growing challenges of organic waste disposal and renewable energy generation. The system automatically detects, segregates, and processes organic waste while extracting biogas for energy production, creating a closed-loop sustainable waste management solution.

The invention combines advanced sensors, real-time monitoring, automated waste segregation, and an integrated biogas extraction mechanism within a smart bin infrastructure. This revolutionary approach not only solves waste management problems but also contributes to renewable energy generation, making it a dual-purpose environmental solution.

Background & Problem Statement

Urban areas worldwide face critical challenges in managing the ever-increasing volume of organic waste. Traditional waste management systems are inefficient, expensive, and environmentally harmful. Key problems include:

  • Over 50% of municipal waste is organic but often ends up in landfills
  • Decomposing organic waste in landfills produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas
  • Manual waste segregation is inconsistent and labor-intensive
  • Limited infrastructure for converting organic waste to useful resources
  • Lack of real-time monitoring in existing waste management systems
  • High operational costs for waste collection and processing

How It Works

Step 1: Waste Detection & Segregation

When waste is deposited, sensors analyze its composition using weight, moisture, and infrared signatures. The AI algorithm classifies it as organic or inorganic, and the mechanical sorter directs it to the appropriate compartment.

Step 2: Organic Waste Processing

Organic waste is transferred to the sealed biogas digestion chamber. The system maintains optimal temperature and moisture levels for anaerobic bacteria to break down the waste efficiently.

Step 3: Biogas Production

Over 15-20 days, anaerobic digestion converts organic matter into biogas (primarily methane). Gas sensors monitor production levels and quality in real-time.

Step 4: Energy Utilization

Generated biogas can be stored in cylinders, used directly for cooking/heating, or converted to electricity through small generators. The system tracks energy output and usage patterns.

Step 5: By-product Management

The solid digestate remaining after biogas extraction is nutrient-rich compost, which can be used as organic fertilizer. This closes the loop on waste management.

Step 6: Smart Monitoring & Alerts

The IoT platform continuously monitors all parameters and sends alerts to users via mobile app. Data analytics provide insights for optimizing waste management practices.
